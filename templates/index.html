Wellcome to Hash chatbot

As part of the Innovation Hub on Artificial Intelligence for Maternal, Sexual, and Reproductive Health in Sub-Saharan Africa (HASH), we are committed to assisting Ethiopian university students with sexually transmitted disease awareness, a start-up diagnostic chatbot, and local parameters contributing the transmission of sexually transmitted diseases (STDs). The chatbot was created to be anonymous, and the awareness generation is based on key and current characteristics of campus life and its surrounds. This initiative supports and contributes to the achievement of SDG3: Good health and well-being.

About Hash chatbot project

The Innovation Hub on Artificial Intelligence for Maternal, Sexual, and Reproductive Health in Sub- Saharan Africa (HASH) is a project supported by the IDRC under the AI4D project. As part of the sub- grantee, a team from Addis Abeba Science and Technology University (AASTU), directed by Surafel Luleseged Tilahun (PhD) as a PI and Mr. Getachew Derso as a co-PI created this site featuring an anonymous chatbot for STD diagnosis from the outset. The team is divided into sub-teams: the mathematical modeling team, directed by Mr. Solomon Molla; the chatbot design team, led by Ms. Mihiret Tibebe and Ms. Elsabeth Solomon; and the medical team, lead by Dr Frehiwot Gutema. This website contains basic project information, an anonymous chatbot, the nearest relevant health centers, surrounding situations and factors, and other resources for raising awareness. For more information, please contact the project's principal investigator at Surafel.luleseged@aastu.edu.et.

General Awareness on STDs Protection/Prevention

Sexually transmitted diseases (STDs) are a significant public health concern, and it is essential to promote awareness and education about their prevention and protection.

Get regular testing:-Even if you don't have symptoms, regular STD testing is important for early detection and treatment.

Seek resources and support: Access reliable sources for information, testing services, and support organizations that can provide guidance and counseling.

  • Reduce risk
  • Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP)
  • Inform sexual partners
  • Vaccinations

Chatbot

The chatbot is designed to provide a preliminary diagnosis and is not intended to replace health experts. It is a rule-based chatbot that is built on a designed expert system.
The chatbot is anonymous and will not collect any personal information from the user, so users can freely utilize it without worry of their responses being linked to them. To use the chatbot, click the "Continue to chatbot" button below:
