Wellcome to Ethio-Hash Project

As part of the Innovation Hub on Artificial Intelligence for Maternal, Sexual, and Reproductive Health in Sub-Saharan Africa (HASH), we are committed to assisting Ethiopian university students with sexually transmitted disease awareness, a start-up diagnostic chatbot, listing nearest relevant health centers, and identifying local parameters contributing the transmission of sexually transmitted diseases (STDs). The chatbot was created to be anonymous, and the awareness generation is based on key and current characteristics of campus life and its surrounds. This initiative supports and contributes to the achievement of SDG3: Good health and well-being.

About the Project

Prevention

Here is a simple ABC approach to STD prevention.

A: Abstinence

The most effective way to prevent STDs is to abstain from sexual activity, including vaginal, anal, and oral sex. Abstinence eliminates the risk of contracting or transmitting STDs.

B: Be Faithful

If you are in a mutually monogamous relationship with a partner who has tested negative for STDs, the risk of contracting STDs is significantly reduced. Being faithful and having a committed, long-term relationship with a partner who is also faithful can help prevent the spread of STDs.

C: Condom Use

Consistent and correct use of condoms can greatly reduce the risk of contracting or transmitting STDs. Condoms act as a barrier and provide protection against many STDs, including HIV, gonorrhea, chlamydia, and syphilis. It's important to use condoms during every sexual encounter, from start to finish, for maximum protection.
In addition to the ABC approach, it's essential to get tested regularly for STDs, especially if you are sexually active or have multiple partners. Testing allows for early detection, timely treatment, and prevention of further transmission. It's also crucial to communicate openly and honestly with your partner(s) about sexual health, STDs, and testing. This helps establish trust and promotes responsible sexual behavior.

Your Surrounding

A mathematical model was developed based on some assumptions. Data was collected from campus clinic as well as surrounding health centers. Further data from the literature was used to fill missing data to analyze the effects of the parameters. Accordingly, the transmission of STD from the surrounding community is higher than that of the in-campus as well as from health centers transmission. The STD transmission within campus is second higher when compared to the transmission from hospitalized or follow up patients. Hence, students need to be aware and protect themselves by following proper measures, using the “ABC” Approach: Abstinence, Being Faithful, and Using Condoms.
